Why now

MCWW is already in the middle of a broader modernization effort. That creates a strong moment to introduce a system that works before, during, and after cloud migration: centralizing knowledge, reducing friction in support, and making revenue-critical information easier to use across teams.

Support & uptime

Reduce time-to-answer and avoid unnecessary escalation

Support agents today are piecing together answers across manuals, file storage, tickets, email, and team conversations. Glean gives them one place to search, ask, and follow citations back to source material.

Engineering capacity

Give product and engineering teams time back

When engineering is repeatedly pulled into support, roadmap work slows down. Glean helps capture those resolutions so the next similar issue can be handled faster without re-creating the same escalation loop.

Growth

Turn scattered revenue data into field and sales leverage

MCWW already sees an opportunity to search historical sales, identify customers due for refresh, prepare for trips more intelligently, and respond to RFPs faster with product-backed answers.

Where the ROI comes from

The business case does not depend on one single bet. It compounds across support productivity, engineering time reclaimed, faster revenue motions, and better documentation reuse.

1. Support productivity

  • Less time searching across systems per case
  • Faster answers for new and mid-tenure reps
  • More consistent responses across software, hardware, and training questions
  • Lower dependence on the same few experts

2. Engineering capacity

  • Fewer repeat escalations for known issues
  • Faster handoff when escalation is required
  • Resolution summaries become reusable knowledge instead of one-off effort
  • More time back for releases, fixes, and roadmap work

3. Installed-base growth

  • Search 20 years of customer history in one place
  • Find accounts with aging equipment or refresh signals
  • Plan higher-yield field trips and account coverage
  • Support better account planning with customer 360 context

4. RFP and quote acceleration

  • Faster retrieval of product dimensions, maintenance details, and documentation
  • Less manual copying from product documents
  • More repeatable draft responses
  • Longer-term path toward configuration and BOM assist

Priority use cases for MCWW

Based on the calls so far, these are the most concrete, customer-relevant workflows to prioritize.

Use case Current challenge Future state with Glean Primary value
Support knowledge copilot Agents search manuals, file storage, tickets, email, and team conversations before escalating. Agents ask one question, get a cited answer, and can continue with follow-up troubleshooting or draft a customer-ready reply. Faster answers, lower escalation volume, better consistency.
Escalation knowledge capture Engineering and senior support often solve issues, but the knowledge does not reliably become reusable documentation. Resolution summaries become part of the searchable knowledge base and can be reused on the next similar case. Reclaimed engineering time and stronger repeatability.
Installed-base search / refresh targeting Historical customer sales are hard to search across legacy and current systems. Sales can search one place for customers with aging equipment, large multi-site footprints, or low recent activity. Better trip planning, upsell identification, and share-of-wallet growth.
Customer 360 for sellers Important account context lives across service issues, transactions, documents, and internal notes. Sellers get a structured view of account history, issues, opportunities, and suggested next steps. More productive prep and stronger customer conversations.
RFP response automation Teams manually hunt through product docs to answer repetitive questions. Glean drafts responses from source material and shows where the answer came from. Shorter response cycles and less manual rework.
Quote / BOM assist The current configurator process is Excel-heavy and separated from the ERP workflow. Longer term, Glean can support product / item lookup and assist with structured configuration workflows where the right data is available. Reduced manual effort and better sales responsiveness.

What the validation session already proved

The strongest part of the story is that this is not just theoretical. The validation session used MCWW content and real test questions from the team.

Question tested What Glean did Why it matters
What size is the crossbeam of the Sudz-A-Lot arch? Returned the specific dimension and cited the source document. Shows exact-answer retrieval from product documentation.
How do I replace my Mac Valve? Returned a step-by-step answer, started with safety guidance, and offered follow-up troubleshooting. Demonstrates support guidance, not just search.
SCF1 error code Recognized it as a troubleshooting query, suggested recommended actions, and asked for more context. Shows guided problem resolution and clarification.
Slow down on time is on what version of Dry N Shine? Resolved the answer from a transcribed video-based source. Expands usable knowledge beyond static manuals.
My buffer top wheel will not come down when I send the ready signal. Mapped customer terminology (“buffer”) to the right product context and assembled an answer from multiple sources. Shows the system can handle real-world phrasing, not just ideal wording.
Why am I not getting reports? Asked for clarification when the question was too vague, then produced a better answer once product context was added. Shows the right balance between precision and guardrails.

Suggested starting point

The fastest path to measurable ROI is to land in support first, prove value quickly, and then expand into revenue workflows using the same underlying knowledge foundation.

Phase 1

Support + escalation knowledge

Connect the highest-value support sources first, validate answers against real workflows, and measure search time, escalations, and engineering interruption.

Phase 2

Sales search + customer 360

Layer in legacy and current sales history so the field team can identify refresh opportunities, prepare for trips, and search account context instantly.

Phase 3

RFP + quote assist

Use the same indexed knowledge to accelerate RFP responses and continue assessing how far product / item data can support quote and BOM-related workflows.

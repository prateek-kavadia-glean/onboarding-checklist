Deployment workspace Work AI that works — rollout planning Glean.com

One calm place to plan, run, and track your rollout.

A shared checklist for your team and Glean: clear stages, plain language, and room to capture what matters—without dumping every detail on the first screen.

What is this? A working deployment guide you can fill in during meetings. It saves in your browser; export anytime for backup or sharing.
Who is it for? Your program lead, technical admins, and Glean’s project team—plus anyone joining mid-flight who needs the story fast.
How do I use it? Complete the snapshot and early alignment, then move top to bottom through the stages. Expand sections when you need depth.
What happens first? Confirm scope, owners, identity (sign-in), and which apps connect first—then stand up the environment and configure access.
Fastest path Smaller scope and quick approvals can reach first meaningful use in about 1–3 weeks, depending mostly on how much content is indexed.
Typical rollout With standard security steps and a focused first wave, many teams plan roughly 4–6 weeks from kickoff to a solid pilot.
Broader rollout More apps, phased audiences, training, and governance reviews stretch the path—often multi-phase over several weeks.

Timing varies with scope, number of connected apps, approval cycles, how you roll out to users, and training needs. Use the stages below—not a single fixed date—as your source of truth.

How we’ll know the launch is on track

Technical readiness

  • Sign-on and people data work for pilot users.
  • First apps are connected and indexing is far enough along to trust results.
  • Results respect existing permissions.

User readiness

  • Champions and first-wave users know how to get in.
  • Browser extension or chosen entry points are rolled out as planned.
  • Helpful starting content exists where you need it (short answers, links, announcements).

Launch readiness

  • Comms and training land with the first audience.
  • Assistant or other AI features match your agreed scope.
  • There’s a clear “day one” experience—not a blank search box.

Feedback & measurement

  • Baseline and follow-up feedback are planned.
  • A simple channel exists for issues and requests.
  • You’ve scheduled a checkpoint to review adoption and next steps.

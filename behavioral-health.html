Executive overview

The market has moved beyond generic teletherapy. Winning models now combine multi-stakeholder workflows, measurement-based care, stronger plan or employer economics, and increasingly, AI-enabled operations.

What changed

Virtual access became table stakes

Most serious vendors now offer some mix of virtual therapy, psychiatry, coaching, and digital content. Differentiation has shifted to outcomes, acuity coverage, integration depth, and buyer economics.

Who controls spend

Payers and systems are gaining leverage

Employers still buy heavily, but parity pressure, utilization growth, and point-solution fatigue are pushing more control toward health plans, provider systems, and integrated ecosystems.

Technology moat

Data + workflow beat simple marketplaces

The strongest platforms combine proprietary routing, care registries, outcomes analytics, and AI-enabled clinician workflows rather than acting as scheduling layers on top of therapist supply.

Segment map

The market is most useful when split by buyer, acuity, and operating model.

Segment 1

Enterprise virtual mental health platforms

Buyer: employers and health plans. Typical wedge: EAP replacement, workforce benefit, or virtual behavioral carve-out. Representative players: Lyra, Spring, Modern Health, Headspace, Talkspace, BetterHelp for Work, Included Health.

Segment 2

High-acuity, SMI/SUD, and value-based specialists

Buyer: Medicaid MCOs, MA plans, risk-bearing providers. Typical wedge: severe mental illness, addiction, dual-diagnosis, and total-cost-of-care reduction. Representative players: Eleanor, Quartet, Cityblock, Amae, Author.

Segment 3

Collaborative-care and primary-care integration

Buyer: health systems, PCP groups, ACOs, and sometimes payers. Typical wedge: CoCM, BH integration billing, psychiatric consult support, and registry-driven care management. Representative players: Concert, Mindoula, Array.

Segment 4

Pediatric and family-focused platforms

Buyer: schools, pediatric practices, employers, and health plans. Typical wedge: youth therapy, psychiatry, parent support, school-based access, and neurodiversity. Representative players: Bend, Hazel, Brightline.

Segment 5

Software-heavy digital therapeutics and AI support

Buyer: employers, plans, and systems. Typical wedge: low-cost first-line treatment, CBT programs, AI companions, and clinician-augmented digital pathways. Representative players: Big Health, Woebot, SilverCloud.

What to watch

Convergence is increasing

Enterprise platforms are moving into pediatrics and specialty care; SMI/SUD vendors are adding analytics and AI; collaborative-care vendors are becoming more payer-facing; and DTx vendors are seeking deeper clinical integration.

1. Enterprise virtual mental health platforms

These companies typically sell to large employers, increasingly to health plans, and sometimes to health systems. Their shared pitch is broad access, measurable outcomes, and workforce-level mental health strategy rather than point-in-time therapy visits.

Lyra Health

Outcomes-driven workforce mental health platform with broad acuity coverage, global reach, and expanding pediatric depth.

Employers Health plans Therapy + psychiatry AI matching

Core product

  • Stepped model across self-guided CBT, coaching, therapy, psychiatry, SUD, and crisis pathways.
  • Increasingly whole-family after Bend acquisition.
  • Acts like a next-gen EAP plus clinical specialty network.

Go-to-market

  • Large self-insured employers first; growing health-plan and health-system presence.
  • Typical sale centers on workforce outcomes, access, and medical spend reduction.
  • Commercial model is usually PEPM/PMPM with guarantees.

Scale

  • 20M+ covered people globally.
  • $900M+ raised and among the best-capitalized players in the segment.
  • Used by major enterprise accounts and increasingly positioned as a category leader.

Technology differentiators

  • AI-powered routing and matching.
  • Mature measurement-based care and outcomes reporting.
  • Lyra Empower adds AI-enabled navigation and operations support.

Spring Health

Precision mental health platform built around machine-learning-based triage, care matching, and employer analytics.

Employers Payers Precision matching Analytics

Core product

  • Stepped model spanning digital content, coaching, therapy, psychiatry, and family care.
  • Strong emphasis on precision assessments and provider matching.
  • Broadening into teens, eating disorders, neurodiversity, and workplace crisis support.

Go-to-market

  • Employer-first with increasing payer partnerships.
  • Buyers are CHROs, benefits leaders, and population health teams.
  • Usually priced as PEPM/PMPM; more enterprise than SMB friendly.

Scale

  • 20M+ people served globally and 450+ large employers.
  • Raised roughly $466M+ with unicorn valuation.
  • High-profile relationships with large enterprises and payer partners like Highmark.

Technology differentiators

  • ML-based assessment and routing.
  • Atlas analytics platform for benefits teams.
  • Notable focus on ethical AI benchmarking through VERA-MH.

Modern Health

Global workforce platform with a coaching-forward adaptive care model and strong DEIB positioning.

Global employers Coaching Therapy + psychiatry Flexible pricing

Core product

  • Adaptive care model across self-guided programs, coaching, therapy, and psychiatry.
  • Built for distributed workforces with strong cultural and language coverage.
  • Often positioned as broader wellbeing plus clinical mental health.

Go-to-market

  • Global multinationals and some health plans.
  • Sells into HR, total rewards, and global wellbeing leaders.
  • More flexible commercial posture than some peers, including usage-based options.

Scale

  • Roughly $170M raised.
  • Known for strong international coverage and equity-oriented messaging.
  • ROI claims emphasize savings from moderate-need populations, not just crisis care.

Technology differentiators

  • Algorithmic routing across care levels.
  • Population analytics by geography, language, and employee segment.
  • Responsible AI and culturally responsive care are key parts of the pitch.

Headspace Health

Consumer-grade engagement engine merged with coaching, therapy, psychiatry, and EAP infrastructure.

EAP replacement Mindfulness Coaching AI companion

Core product

  • Mindfulness and sleep content from Headspace, layered with Ginger’s 24/7 coaching and clinical care.
  • Therapy, psychiatry, and work-life services wrapped into a single enterprise offering.
  • Works well as a prevention-heavy, engagement-led front door.

Go-to-market

  • Sells to employers, health plans, consultants, and SMBs.
  • Strong fit where brand recognition and engagement matter as much as clinical depth.
  • Often used as a broad wellbeing plus BH bundle.

Scale

  • 4,000+ organizational clients and 190+ country reach.
  • Among the best-known mental health technology brands in the world.
  • Backed by a deep research record and large enterprise footprint.

Technology differentiators

  • Ebb AI companion for triage and support.
  • Deep content library and consumer-grade engagement mechanics.
  • Large evidence base across mindfulness and digital mental health outcomes.

Talkspace

Messaging-first therapy and psychiatry platform with both DTC and employer/payer distribution.

Messaging therapy Public company Employers Plans

Core product

  • Asynchronous therapy via messaging, with optional live video and telepsychiatry.
  • Supports individuals, couples, and teens.
  • Best suited to convenience and accessibility rather than deep stepped-care orchestration.

Go-to-market

  • Hybrid DTC + B2B motion.
  • Sold into employers, plans, and brokers as an EAP alternative or digital therapy benefit.
  • Competes on access, convenience, and lower-friction therapy engagement.

Scale

  • National coverage in all 50 states.
  • Large brand recognition from consumer business.
  • Public company status increases transparency but also market pressure.

Technology differentiators

  • Mature asynchronous therapist workflows.
  • Platform designed around text-based engagement at scale.
  • Published data supports improvements in symptoms and work productivity.

BetterHelp for Work

Mass-scale virtual therapy network adapted for employers and plans with usage-based enterprise pricing.

Usage-based Teladoc Therapy network Access play

Core product

  • Primarily virtual therapy with messaging between visits.
  • Adults, couples, and teens; lighter on high-acuity orchestration than Lyra or Spring.
  • Very strong therapist supply and broad consumer familiarity.

Go-to-market

  • Dual DTC + enterprise motion via Teladoc ecosystem.
  • Enterprise wedge is simplicity and cost control rather than deep clinical infrastructure.
  • Usage-based pricing is an important commercial differentiator.

Scale

  • One of the largest online therapy brands in the world.
  • Embedded inside Teladoc’s broader virtual care portfolio.
  • Frequently appears in employer BH shortlists due to brand and reach.

Technology differentiators

  • Operational scale in virtual therapist supply.
  • Platform well-optimized for matching, scheduling, and message-based care.
  • Less differentiated on employer analytics or measurement-based care than top enterprise peers.

Included Health

Navigation-first virtual care platform that blends behavioral health with primary care, advocacy, and specialty guidance.

Navigation Primary + BH Employers Plans

Core product

  • Single front door for virtual primary care, behavioral health, navigation, billing support, and expert opinions.
  • Behavioral health is one pillar within a broader care and navigation stack.
  • Appeals to buyers that want fewer point solutions.

Go-to-market

  • Large employers, health plans, public sector, and labor groups.
  • Sold as a unified navigation and care layer rather than a standalone behavioral vendor.
  • Competitive whenever medical + behavioral integration matters.

Scale

  • Millions of members across 300+ partners.
  • National footprint across all 50 states.
  • Strong enterprise credibility from legacy Grand Rounds + Doctor on Demand roots.

Technology differentiators

  • Claims, navigation, and virtual-care data integrated into one experience.
  • Multi-service orchestration is the key moat.
  • Less specialized in behavioral depth, stronger in front-door experience and benefit navigation.

2. High-acuity, SMI/SUD, and value-based specialists

These companies focus on serious mental illness, substance use disorder, and other high-cost populations. Their most common buyers are Medicaid MCOs, Medicare Advantage plans, and risk-bearing providers. The central economic claim is reduced ED use, inpatient utilization, or total cost of care.

Eleanor Health

Value-based addiction and mental health provider built around longitudinal, whole-person recovery.

SUD Value-based Medicaid / MA Hybrid clinics

Core product

  • Hybrid SUD + mental health model with MAT, therapy, psychiatry, peers, nursing, and SDOH support.
  • Harm-reduction oriented and explicitly built for co-occurring physical and behavioral complexity.
  • Longitudinal relationship model rather than episodic rehab.

Go-to-market

  • National and regional Medicaid, MA, and commercial payer contracts.
  • Strong value-based orientation with PMPM/shared-savings structures.
  • Also acts as a high-acuity backend for broader digital mental health partners.

Scale

  • Roughly $130M+ raised across multiple rounds.
  • 17+ payer contracts across several core states.
  • Published outcomes focus on large reductions in ED and inpatient utilization.

Technology differentiators

  • Population analytics for attribution and risk identification.
  • Measurement-based care tracking for cravings, symptoms, and longitudinal recovery.
  • Value-based reporting infrastructure is core, not peripheral.

Valera Health

Virtual psychiatric medical group serving mild-to-SMI populations, increasingly deep in MA and suicide prevention.

Virtual psychiatry SMI MA / Medicaid In-network

Core product

  • Therapy, group therapy, psychiatry, and medication management for adults and children.
  • Handles higher-acuity populations that many digital BH vendors avoid.
  • Post-Vita acquisition, deeper into suicide prevention and high-risk youth.

Go-to-market

  • Primarily health plans, universities, and provider partners.
  • Strong Medicaid and Medicare Advantage orientation.
  • Core business remains in-network FFS with growing VBC ambitions.

Scale

  • 37M+ covered lives through health-plan relationships.
  • $79M+ raised.
  • Humana MA and Point32Health relationships are strategically important.

Technology differentiators

  • Virtual care platform designed for coordinated therapist + psychiatrist workflows.
  • Measurement-based care and quality metrics for higher-acuity panels.
  • Less about consumer UX, more about network adequacy and specialty capacity.

aptihealth

Virtual higher-acuity behavioral platform embedded into regional health systems and payer workflows.

Health systems ED discharge Higher acuity Workflow platform

Core product

  • Virtual therapy, psychiatry, and care coordination for higher-acuity depression, anxiety, SMI, and SUD.
  • Often activated at transition points like ED discharge or inpatient step-down.
  • Works as a specialty layer for health systems short on BH capacity.

Go-to-market

  • Regional health plans and health systems first.
  • Embedded referral and EHR integration are central to the sale.
  • Mix of FFS and performance-based arrangements.

Scale

  • $65M+ raised.
  • Partnerships with CDPHP, Albany Med, St. Peter’s, and other Northeast players.
  • Often strongest in markets where system-level BH bottlenecks are acute.

Technology differentiators

  • Referral, triage, and collaborative care workflows built for health system integration.
  • Evidence-based assessments and longitudinal outcomes tracking.
  • Technology moat is operational workflow depth rather than marketplace scale.

Quartet Health

Behavioral care enablement platform evolving into a full-risk SMI and whole-health provider.

SMI Full-risk Matching engine Payers

Core product

  • Started with identification and matching into in-network BH care.
  • Now operates SMI clinic services and Whole Health programs that integrate physical, behavioral, and social care.
  • Bridges network enablement and direct care delivery.

Go-to-market

  • Health plans, systems, CMHCs, and FQHCs across 39 states.
  • Legacy contracts focused on access and network activation.
  • Newer models include full-risk MA SMI partnerships like Clover Health.

Scale

  • 20,000+ external providers and 200+ clinicians in its own group.
  • 500,000+ patients touched annually.
  • More than $200M raised historically and now part of NeuroFlow.

Technology differentiators

  • Claims + EHR-based identification and referral workflows.
  • Measurement-based care and value-based analytics are central.
  • One of few players combining enablement infrastructure with SMI risk-bearing care.

Cityblock Health

Full-stack, full-risk primary + behavioral + social care platform for Medicaid and dually eligible populations.

Full-risk Medicaid / duals Community care LTSS

Core product

  • Integrated medical, behavioral, and SDOH support through neighborhood hubs, home visits, and virtual care.
  • Advanced Behavioral Health program addresses SMI and SUD inside the broader model.
  • Designed for the highest-cost, hardest-to-engage Medicaid and dual cohorts.

Go-to-market

  • Capitated or globally budgeted contracts with Medicaid MCOs and duals plans.
  • Health-plan and system partnerships often anchor new-market launches.
  • Sold as a whole-person population health engine, not a BH point solution.

Scale

  • 100,000+ members across 10+ states.
  • Over $1B in annual revenue and roughly $886M raised.
  • One of the largest and most ambitious payvider models in the space.

Technology differentiators

  • Claims, SDOH, field, and clinical data integrated into one operating platform.
  • AI and ML for segmentation, prioritization, and emerging LTSS workflows.
  • Technology is built to support distributed, community-based teams at scale.

firsthand

Peer-led, field-based serious mental illness model built for total-cost-of-care contracts in Medicaid.

SMI Peers Field outreach TCoC

Core product

  • Peer-majority field teams supported by psych-savvy NPs and care staff.
  • Designed for unengaged, high-cost SMI patients in homes, shelters, and community settings.
  • Handles behavioral, physical, and social needs together.

Go-to-market

  • Medicaid managed care organizations are the core buyer.
  • Contracts emphasize total-cost accountability and engagement of the hardest-to-reach members.
  • Commercial story is limited; payer dependency is high.

Scale

  • Raised roughly $43M including GV support.
  • Notable partnerships with Carelon and others.
  • Contract volatility has already shown how fragile the model can be operationally.

Technology differentiators

  • Purpose-built CRM and analytics for peer workflows and payer targeting.
  • Data-driven case targeting and engagement management.
  • Differentiation is less about flashy AI and more about tech supporting field operations.

Amae Health

Clinic-first SMI center of excellence combining high-touch psychiatry with precision data infrastructure.

SMI Clinic-first Precision platform Health systems

Core product

  • In-person intensive SMI programs for psychosis, bipolar disorder, TRD, and severe mood disorders.
  • Strong integration of psychiatry, primary care, therapy, coaching, peers, and life goals.
  • Much more center-of-excellence than marketplace or teletherapy platform.

Go-to-market

  • Payers plus academic and major health systems.
  • Warm-handoff partnerships from EDs and inpatient psych units are critical.
  • Value story centers on readmission reduction and sustained stabilization.

Scale

  • More than $50M raised across multiple rounds.
  • Clinics in Los Angeles, New York, and North Carolina with expansion plans.
  • Strong institutional partnerships with Cedars-Sinai, Stanford, and others.

Technology differentiators

  • Palantir-based precision platform aggregating EHR, labs, PROMs, and wearables.
  • AI support for patient-at-a-glance views and risk detection.
  • More biologically and clinically sophisticated than most digital BH peers.

Medicare-focused behavioral health specialist for older adults with SMI, dementia, and substance use disorder.

Medicare Advantage Seniors Dementia Value-based

Core product

  • Virtual-first psychiatry, therapy, nursing, and community support for complex Medicare populations.
  • Targets SMI, dementia, and SUD in MA and SNP populations.

  • Differentiated more by population focus than by consumer-facing product polish.

3. Primary care and collaborative-care integration

This segment is defined by embedded behavioral health: collaborative care, psychiatric consult support, registry-driven workflows, BHI billing, and integration into PCP and hospital systems.

Concert Health

Turnkey collaborative-care medical group for primary care, women’s health, and pediatrics.

CoCM Primary care Medical group Registry

Core product

  • Behavioral care managers and psychiatric consultants embedded into PCP workflows.
  • Evidence-based collaborative care with treat-to-target measurement.
  • Acts as a managed BH extension of primary care rather than a separate destination.

Go-to-market

  • Health systems, large medical groups, and some payer-sponsored networks.
  • Commercial fit is strongest where CoCM reimbursement can be operationalized at scale.
  • Often sold as a turnkey service line with implementation and revenue-cycle support.

Scale

  • 150,000+ patients cared for.
  • 350+ masters-level clinicians and 20+ psychiatric consultants.
  • Operates across 19 states with major system relationships.

Technology differentiators

  • Registry and outcomes workflows embedded into referring EMRs.
  • Measurement-based care is baked into operations and reporting.
  • High operational maturity around CoCM implementation.

Mindoula

Population-health BH partner combining virtual collaborative care overlays with high-risk Medicaid programs.

Population health CoCM Medicaid Predictive analytics

Core product

  • Virtual collaborative-care overlay for PCPs plus specialty programs for high-risk populations.
  • Behavioral managers, psychiatric consultants, 24/7 support, and app-based engagement.
  • Straddles primary-care integration and payer-facing population management.

Go-to-market

  • Health plans, health systems, hospitals, and PCP groups.
  • Strong fit in Medicaid and complex social-risk populations.
  • Uses ROI framing rather than pure access messaging.

Scale

  • Active in 23 states plus DC.
  • Raised ~$31M.
  • Large partnership with Community Health Systems brings BH support to hundreds of PCP sites.

Technology differentiators

  • Mindoula registry and M3 screening tools.
  • Predictive analytics for risk stratification and targeted interventions.
  • Interoperability via marketplaces like athenahealth improves deployment speed.

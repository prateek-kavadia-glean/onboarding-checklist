Built for an exec read: short, evidence-backed, no fluff.
1 · Architecture

Glean is built around enterprise context.

Glean’s product story is not “one model with some tools.” It is a horizontal AI platform built on connectors, indexed search, Enterprise Graph, and enterprise memory so agents can work with company reality.

  • Connectors capture enterprise signals.
  • Search indexes data for fast retrieval.
  • Enterprise Graph maps relationships across people, content, customers, and products.
  • Enterprise memory learns how work gets done over time.
2 · Governance

Glean is stronger if trust and security are board-level concerns.

Glean publicly emphasizes running in a single-tenant cloud, enforcing strict permissions, and validating every agent action. That is a better executive story when the buying criteria are scale, governance, and safe deployment.

  • Permissions-aware retrieval and actioning.
  • Referenceable answers and action validation.
  • A neutral layer above models rather than dependence on one model vendor.
3 · Breadth

Glean is the better fit for the messy reality of enterprise sprawl.

Glean says it supports 100+ connectors and positions itself as the context layer beneath multiple models and apps. That matters when an enterprise spans collaboration, CRM, support, engineering, and internal knowledge systems.

  • Cross-app retrieval is the product core.
  • Model-agnostic approach reduces lock-in risk.
  • Enterprise context becomes reusable across assistants and agents.
Public quality signal

The clearest public head-to-head favors Glean.

Glean published a blind evaluation in Feb 2026 using ~280 complex enterprise queries over comparable connected data sources. In the Claude comparison, Glean and Claude both used Sonnet 4.5, isolating the search/context stack rather than giving Glean a model advantage.

Interpretation

Why Glean responses are stronger

  • Better odds of finding the canonical source across systems.
  • Better handling of company language, ambiguity, and relationships.
  • Less reliance on brute-force tool calling to compensate for weak retrieval.
  • More complete answers across query types including analyze, decide, draft, learn, and locate.
Important caveat

Use the benchmark honestly.

The 1.6× figure is a valuable but vendor-published signal, not an independent analyst bake-off. The right exec framing is: Glean has published the strongest concrete public evidence we found that its enterprise answer quality beats Claude enterprise search when using comparable data and the same model family.

Claude strengths

Claude Cowork is compelling when the buyer wants a Claude-centered workflow layer.

  • Private plugin marketplaces for internal distribution.
  • Role-based plugins for HR, engineering, operations, finance, and research workflows.
  • Admin controls, provisioning, and observability improvements.
  • Cross-app Excel ↔ PowerPoint workflow support in research preview.
  • Scheduled tasks and newer computer-use style capabilities in Cowork.
Trade-off

But Claude’s enterprise story is still more plugin-first than context-first.

Claude Cowork has clearly accelerated in 2026, but its public story emphasizes connectors, plugins, and admin surfaces around Claude. Glean’s public story is deeper on the persistent enterprise context layer itself — which is usually the harder problem in large deployments.

Best exec framing

If the customer asks “Why not just buy Claude?”

  • If you want a great model with growing workflow plugins, Claude Cowork is credible.
  • If you want the enterprise context substrate that makes any model more trustworthy across your business, Glean is the better strategic buy.
  • Glean can also bring its context into other model ecosystems rather than forcing a single-vendor bet.
